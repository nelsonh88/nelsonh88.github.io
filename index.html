About Me

I am a full stack web developer who is motivated, driven, and a compassionate individual that will continue to grow in the web development field. In January 2018 I made a career change from the physical therapy/ health field to the web development field. After months of recommendations from friends and along with my own research in the field I decided to jump right into the field and enroll into a web development immersive program at General Assembly. I had absolutely no prior experience in the web development field when I enrolled into the 3-month immersive program at General Assembly. I love the freedom of the field to explore different avenues in web development and that it supports my entrepreneurial drive. I would love to work in a company that allows me to grow and learn as a full stack web developer and eventually become a leader in the field! I am excited for this new chapter of my life and to see where my web development journey will take me. Are you ready to be part of this journey with me?

Geo-Footprint

For my second project and my first full-stack project ever I decided to build a traveling log called Geo-Footprint where users can store their trips and see their geo-footprints. A user needs to sign in or sign up if not already a user. Once signed in a user can view their past trips if any and can also record a trip with their trip details. A user can CRUD on their trips resource. The back end was made using Ruby, Ruby on Rails, and RESTful routes for handling requests. The front end was made using HTML, CSS/SASS, JavaScript, jQuery, and AJAX. I also challenged myself to make Geo-Footprint responsive so it can be used on mobile devices.

Jump Ahead

For my third project and first group project I had to work in a group with four other developers to make a content management system (CMS) application where companies/organizations that want to have their own company blog and web pages and can CRUD their own "blog posts" and "web pages". We created the CMS application called 'Jump-Ahead' where companies who want to get a jump-ahead on building a strong web presence can come to. The back end was made using MongoDB, Mongoose, and Express. The front end was made using HTML, CSS/SASS, JavaScript, jQuery, Handlebars and AJAX. Third party API Stripe intergrated.

MotivationQ

For my fourth and final project at General Assembly I decided to make a full-stack application called MotivationQ. MotivationQ is a community where users can motivate each other by storing their favorite motivational quotes and share it with other users. If a user likes another user's motivational quote they can add that quote to their own list. MotivationQ's API was made using Ruby and Ruby on Rails and the front end was made using HTML, CSS/SASS, JavaScript, jQuery, Handlebars and AJAX. I also challenged myself to make MotivationQ responsive.
